About

I'm a PhD candidate in Computer Science at UTSA, working at the intersection of Large Language Models and healthcare. My research tackles one of AI's hardest open problems: how do we trust a model that confidently hallucinates?

Before starting my PhD, I spent three years as a Software Engineer shipping 10+ production Android apps and a Vue 3 platform serving 500K+ users. That engineering foundation shapes how I approach research — I care about systems that actually work, not just benchmark wins.

Currently I explore multi-agent evaluation frameworks, causal rule extraction, and explainable AI (Shapley, LIME) for clinical question answering under Dr. Ke Yang at the CAREAI Lab.
